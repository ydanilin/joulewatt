Problem 1.1.5.

Problem text:

Assuming that in an ideal Si diode \(e^{v_D/\eta V_t} \gg 1\). Than:
  • (a) what is the change in vD that multiplies iD by a factor k?
  • (b) what is the change in vD that doubles iD assuming η = 2?
  • (c) what is the change in vD that multiplies iD by a factor of 10 assuming:
    • (i) η = 2, and
    • (ii) η = 1.
  • (d) If ΔvD is the change in vD that causes a 10-fold change in iD, the diode is referred to as a ΔvD ∕ decade diode. What is η for a 0.1 V ∕ decade diode?

Solution:

(a):
As we know from P 1.1.2, very often it is worth to drop off "1" in the Main Diode Formula. To derive the k ratio, let's construct a quotient
